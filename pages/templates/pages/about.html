The History of VolMUN

The first iteration of VolMUN began 26 years ago at the University of Tennessee as a student project. Wanting to understand the diplomatic side of international politics, a group of UT students congregated together to simulate sessions of the United Nations. They assigned themselves countries to represent and debated problems facing them at the time. This led to the first Model UN committee held on campus. These first students wanted to share their experiences with others wishing to learn.

Soon, they expanded their organization by inviting new members to participate in their simulations. Thus, Model United Nations at the University of Tennessee was born. However, it did not stop there. University of Tennessee High School Model United Nations, or UTHSMUN, was created shortly after. It hoped to extend the experience to high school students eager to learn about international affairs and diplomacy.

UTHSMUN catered to local schools and sought to show the next generation of students the value of diplomatic resolve and problem solving. Nevertheless, just like how geopolitical topics evolve, so did our conference. UTHSMUN transformed into VolMUN to better embody the future of Model UN.


VolMUN Today

VolMUN implements innovations in simulated debate to help participants learn about global politics. We practice new methods to develop productive discourse while appreciating the differences that make us unique. VolMUN celebrates an environment of inclusion and diversity that moves towards cooperation and activism. VolMUN is in its 5th iteration and we can't wait to make it the best one!
